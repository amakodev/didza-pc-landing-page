# PC Shop Business Context for ViralFlow Agent

## 1. Business Overview

### Working Brand Position
A Cape Town-based refurbished PC shop focused on affordable, optimized, ready-to-use computers for everyday people, students, households, small businesses, freelancers, and entry-level gamers.

The business buys, refurbishes, optimizes, customizes, and resells PCs. The focus is not just on selling hardware, but on giving customers a complete computer experience that feels simple, safe, affordable, and ready from day one.

The main value is not “cheap PCs.”  
The main value is:

> **Affordable computers that are cleaned, optimized, preinstalled, customized, and ready to use immediately.**

Most competitors sell second-hand PCs as basic hardware.  
This brand sells **peace of mind, convenience, setup, performance tuning, and usefulness.**

---

## 2. Core Business Model

### What We Sell
Entry-level refurbished desktop PCs and selected custom setups.

Current starting stock:
- Intel Core i3 computers
- 4GB RAM
- Suitable for basic daily use
- Starting from **R999**

### What We Do Before Selling
Every PC should be positioned as:
- Refurbished
- Cleaned
- Tested
- Optimized
- Preinstalled
- Ready to use
- Beginner-friendly
- Customizable depending on customer needs

### Main Service Advantage
The major selling point is the **software experience**.

We do not just hand someone a PC and expect them to figure it out. We prepare the machine so the customer can immediately start using it for what they need.

Examples:
- Windows setup
- Basic apps installed
- Browser installed
- Office/productivity tools installed where applicable
- PDF reader
- Media player
- Antivirus/security basics
- Performance optimization
- Desktop cleanup
- Useful shortcuts
- Optional school/work setup
- Optional business setup
- Optional gaming setup
- Optional child/student-friendly setup
- Optional WhatsApp Web/browser setup
- Optional email setup guidance

The product should feel like:

> “Take it home, plug it in, and start using it.”

## 3. Target Market

## Primary Target Audience

### 1. People Who Need a Computer but Do Not Care About Specs
These customers do not want to compare processors, RAM, generations, SSDs, GPUs, or technical details.

They want to know:
- Can it work?
- Can I afford it?
- Can my child use it for school?
- Can I use it for documents?
- Can I use the internet?
- Can I watch videos?
- Can I apply for jobs?
- Can I use email?
- Can I print documents?
- Will someone help me understand what I am buying?

They respond better to practical outcomes than technical specifications.

Do not lead with:
> “Intel i3, 4GB RAM”

Lead with:
> “A ready-to-use computer for schoolwork, job applications, typing, browsing, emails, videos, and everyday tasks — from R999.”

Specs can appear later for credibility, but the emotional buying reason should come first.

## 10. Sales Psychology Principles

## 1. Pain Realization
Make people realize the cost of not having a computer.

Examples:
- “Still typing CVs on your phone?”
- “Still sending your child to borrow a computer?”
- “Still paying internet café costs?”
- “Still struggling with documents on a small screen?”
- “Still running your small business from only WhatsApp on your phone?”

The goal is not to shame them. The goal is to make the problem visible.

---

## 2. Simplicity
The customer must instantly understand the offer.

Good:
> “Basic ready-to-use PCs for school, work, browsing, CVs and documents — from R999.”

Bad:
> “Intel Core i3 refurbished desktops with 4GB RAM and optimized Windows environments.”

---

## 3. Trust Before Selling
Because refurbished PCs can feel risky, trust must come early.

Use phrases like:
- Cleaned
- Tested
- Optimized
- Ready-to-use
- Beginner-friendly
- Honest specs
- Local Cape Town seller
- WhatsApp support
- Stock photos and real photos where possible

---

## 4. FOMO
Use scarcity honestly.

Examples:
- “Entry stock is limited.”
- “R999 starter PCs available while stock lasts.”
- “Once this batch is gone, the next batch may be priced differently.”
- “Cape Town buyers only for this first batch.”
- “First batch opening on WhatsApp.”

Avoid fake pressure. Keep urgency believable.

---

## 5. Urgency
Use time-sensitive calls to action.

Examples:
- “Message us today to see available units.”
- “Ask for the R999 starter list.”
- “Send ‘PC’ on WhatsApp and we’ll show what’s available.”
- “Reserve before the weekend.”
- “Available for this first Cape Town batch.”

---

## 6. No-Brainer Decision Making
Reduce decision friction.

Instead of asking customers to choose between specs, ask:
> “What do you need the PC for?”

Then guide them:
- Schoolwork
- Job applications
- Business admin
- Home use
- Gaming
- Office work

This makes the business feel helpful and consultative.

---

## 7. Competitor Shadowing
Do not insult competitors directly.

Instead, position against weak buying experiences:
- “Not just second-hand hardware.”
- “Not a random PC with no setup.”
- “Not confusing specs and no guidance.”
- “Not a machine you still need to figure out after buying.”

Strong comparison line:
> “Anyone can sell you a second-hand computer. We prepare it for what you actually need it to do.”

---

## 22. Sales Objection Handling

### Objection: “Is 4GB RAM enough?”
Response:
> For basic use like documents, browsing, email, CVs, schoolwork and YouTube, yes, as long as the PC is properly optimized. It is not meant for heavy gaming or advanced editing, but it works well for everyday tasks.
